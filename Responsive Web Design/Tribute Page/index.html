Charles Chamberland French biologist and physicist

Physicist and biologist, Charles Chamberland was one of Louis Pasteur's oldest collaborators. Associated with many works of his master, he was also a genius inventor, designing an autoclave and a water filter that bear his name.

Here's a time line of Charles Chamberland's life:

  • 12 mars 1851 Born in Chilly-le-Vignoble (Jura).
  • 1871-1874 Studies at the École normale supérieure.
  • 1874-1875 Aggregate of physical sciences. Teaches at the Nîmes high school.
  • 1875-1879 Joined Louis Pasteur’s laboratory at the École normale supérieure, rue d'Ulm in Paris. Pasteur associates his name with a publication: The theory of germs and its applications to medicine and surgery.
  • 1878-1880 Studied with Émile Roux the mode of infection of herds by the anthrax bacteria. Shows the existence of a refractory state in animals cured of a first attack. Seeks to produce this condition by inoculating a mild form of the disease.
  • 1879 Doctoral thesis in physical sciences: Research on the origin and development of microscopic organisms. Work on the sterilization of culture media and design of a disinfection oven: the Chamberland autoclave.
  • 1879-1888 Deputy Director of Louis Pasteur's laboratory.
  • 1881 Pasteur is successfully leading a public anti-anthrax vaccination experiment on sheep in Pouilly-le-Fort, with a vaccine developed by Chamberland.
  • 1884 Creates a filter that eliminates microbes from drinking water to fight the typhoid fever that is raging in Paris (Chamberland Filter - Pasteur System).
  • 1885-1889 Elected deputy for Jura; participates in the first public hygiene bill. In 1887, was elected mayor of Chilly-le-Vignoble.
  • 1886-1888 Contributes to the design and organization of the Institut Pasteur.
  • 1888-1904 Appointed head of the Applied Hygiene and Vaccines Microbics Department at the Institut Pasteur.
  • 1900 Opens a factory of wooden boxes for shipping vaccines around the world.
  • 1904-1908 Deputy Director of the Institut Pasteur.
  • 2 mai 1908 Death in Paris.
